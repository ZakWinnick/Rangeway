Building Rangeway: Complete YouTube Series Playbook

A comprehensive guide to documenting the journey of building Rangeway Energy from concept to operation, building community and establishing thought leadership in EV infrastructure.

Series Overview

Series Goal: Document the entire journey of building Rangeway Energy from concept to operation, building community and trust while establishing Zak as a thought leader in EV infrastructure.

Target Audience

  • EV owners and enthusiasts
  • Aspiring infrastructure entrepreneurs
  • Investors interested in cleantech/mobility
  • Potential customers and brand advocates
  • Industry professionals

Posting Cadence

Every 2 weeks initially, scaling to weekly once construction begins

Platform Strategy

  • YouTube: Long-form 10-15 min episodes (primary)
  • YouTube Shorts: 60-90 sec key moments
  • Instagram Reels: Same as YouTube Shorts
  • LinkedIn: Business/founder insights
  • Email Newsletter: Episode recaps with deeper context

Production Notes

Cinematography Style:

  • Premium, cinematic look (think tech company product launch)
  • Wide establishing shots, intimate close-ups
  • Golden hour lighting whenever possible
  • Smooth camera movements (gimbal/drone)
  • Color grade toward warm tones (Sunset Coral/Golden Highway)

Music:

  • Build from ambient/minimal to inspiring/epic
  • Think: Inspiring piano → building strings → triumphant finish
  • Licensed music from Artlist, Epidemic Sound, or Musicbed
  • Or commission original score for brand consistency

Pacing:

  • First 5 seconds: Grab attention (problem hook)
  • Middle 45 seconds: Build excitement (vision & journey)
  • Final 20 seconds: Create urgency (stakes & CTA)
  • Keep cuts tight - no shot longer than 3-4 seconds

Voiceover Recording:

  • Record in quiet space with good acoustics
  • Warm, conversational tone (not announcer voice)
  • Vary pacing: slower for emotion, faster for energy
  • Record multiple takes, cherry-pick best lines
  • Leave pauses for music/visual moments

Critical B-Roll Shots Needed

  • ✅ Scenic corridor drone shot (opening hero shot)
  • ✅ You at future site location with plans/maps
  • ✅ Current broken/bad charging stations
  • ✅ Concept renders or sketches of Basecamp
  • ✅ You working: whiteboard, laptop, meetings
  • ✅ Advisor meetings (even if just establishing shots)
  • ✅ Sunset/golden hour shot of site location
  • ✅ You direct to camera in good lighting
  • ✅ Logo animation (commission from designer)

Multiple Version Strategy

Version A: Full Trailer (90 seconds)

  • YouTube pre-roll before Episode 1
  • Channel trailer for new visitors
  • LinkedIn/Twitter video posts

Version B: Social Teaser (60 seconds)

  • Trim to fastest pacing
  • Instagram/TikTok/YouTube Shorts vertical format (9:16)
  • Add captions (80% watch without sound)
  • End CTA: "Full series starts [date]"

Version C: Investor Cut (90 seconds)

  • Same footage, different emphasis
  • More business metrics on screen
  • Highlight advisor credentials
  • End CTA: "Join us. [email]"

Version D: 15-Second Tease

  • Just the hook + logo + date
  • For social media ads
  • Drive to full trailer or Episode 1

Distribution Strategy

Timeline:

  • 2 weeks before Episode 1: Post full trailer on YouTube
  • 10 days before: Social teaser versions across all platforms
  • 1 week before: 15-second ads on social (if budget allows)
  • 3 days before: Email blast to personal network with trailer
  • Launch day: Pin trailer to YouTube channel, link from Episode 1

Where to Post:

  • YouTube: Main video + pinned to channel
  • Instagram: Reel (9:16 vertical) + Story
  • TikTok: Full vertical version
  • LinkedIn: Full version for professional audience
  • Twitter/X: Cropped for timeline

Episode 2: "How Rangeway Actually Works"

12-15 minutes

Goal: Show this is a real business with solid economics, not just a dream

Act 1: The Two-Site Model (0:30-3:30)

Basecamps: Full-service hubs

  • 6-8 chargers, 2,500-3,000 sq ft lounge
  • Café, premium restrooms, work zones
  • Regional maintenance center + NOC
  • 99% uptime commitment, 35-55 min dwell time

Waystations: Automated excellence

  • 4 chargers (8 ports), 800-1,000 sq ft lounge
  • Automated vending, clean restrooms
  • Remote monitoring, no staff, 98% uptime
  • Maintained by Basecamp teams

Regional Cluster Strategy:

  • 1 Basecamp + 3 Waystations
  • ~2 hour mobile response time
  • "Sites support each other - it's an ecosystem"

Act 2: The Economics (3:30-7:30)

Basecamp Economics:

  • CapEx: $5-7M per site
  • Revenue at maturity: ~$90K/month
  • Payback: 48-60 months
  • 60-80 DCFC sessions/day by Year 3

Waystation Economics:

  • CapEx: $2-3M per site
  • Revenue at maturity: ~$30K/month
  • Payback: 24-36 months
  • Year 1 charging revenue: $1,022,000
  • Year 1 electricity cost: $408,800
  • Year 1 net: $613,200

Regional Cluster at Maturity:

  • Total: $180K/month = $2.16M annually
  • Total CapEx: $11-16M
  • Payback: 36-42 months

Revenue Streams (Priority Order):

  1. Per-kWh charging fees (primary - $0.50/kWh retail)
  2. Café and retail at Basecamps (40% attachment rate)
  3. Digital advertising ($5-10K/month per site at maturity)
  4. Pathfinder Rewards memberships
  5. EV app roaming integrations

Act 3: Operational Secret Sauce (7:30-10:30)

Technology Stack:

  • Kempower Satellite charging systems (modular, reliable)
  • Clockwork Energy (real-time monitoring, predictive maintenance)
  • ChargeMate (AI driver support)
  • ChargeEye CPMS

The Monitoring System:

  • Basecamp NOC monitors all regional sites 24/7
  • Predictive maintenance reduces emergency repairs 50-70%
  • Mobile response vehicles stationed at Basecamps
  • "We don't wait for things to break"

Act 4: The Loyalty Moat (10:30-13:00)

  • Not gas station rewards - "travel lifestyle program"
  • 4 tiers: Explorer, Voyager, Pioneer, Trailblazer
  • Year 5 projection: $935K+ in membership revenue
