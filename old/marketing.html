Part 2: Visual Identity System

Logo Elements

Icon Components

The Rangeway icon captures the essence of scenic journey experiences:

  • Mountain Silhouette: Represents America's diverse landscapes from coastal ranges to mountain passes
  • Winding Path: Dual meaning—the road's journey and flowing natural elements
  • Sun: Golden hour optimism, warmth, and solar power
  • Rounded Square Format: Modern, app-friendly, approachable

Logo Variations

  • Icon Only: For app icons, social media profiles, wayfinding markers
  • Icon + Wordmark: Primary lockup for most applications
  • Wordmark Only: For horizontal space constraints

Typography

Wordmark

  • Font: Bold sans-serif (Montserrat Bold or Raleway Bold)
  • Weight: 700-800
  • Tracking: Generous (approximately 0.08em)
  • Case: All capitals for wordmark

Recommended Type System

  • Headlines: Same as wordmark font family, various weights
  • Body Copy: Clean sans-serif (Source Sans Pro, Open Sans, or similar)
  • Wayfinding: High-legibility sans-serif (Fira Sans, Roboto)
  • Digital: System fonts for fast loading, branded fonts for key moments

Logo Usage Guidelines

Clear Space
  • Minimum clear space = height of the "R" in RANGEWAY
  • Icon should have clear space equal to 25% of its width on all sides
Minimum Sizes
  • Icon only: 32px digital, 0.5" print
  • Full logo: 120px digital, 1.5" print
  • Wordmark only: 80px digital, 1" print
Color Applications
  • Primary: Sunset Coral background with Mountain Charcoal elements
  • Reversed: White logo on dark backgrounds
  • Monochrome: All Mountain Charcoal for single-color needs
Incorrect Usage
  • ❌ Don't alter the colors outside brand palette
  • ❌ Don't rotate or skew the logo
  • ❌ Don't separate the path from the mountain
  • ❌ Don't add effects (shadows, gradients, outlines)
  • ❌ Don't change the typeface
  • ❌ Don't alter proportions

Motion Principles

When animated, the logo should:

  • Sun: Gentle rise or glow
  • Path: Draw in from left to right (journey motion)
  • Mountain: Remain stable (reliability)
  • Overall: Smooth, confident transitions (no bouncing or playful movements)

Photography Style

  • Golden hour shots along America's scenic corridors
  • Authentic moments with real drivers (not models)
  • Vehicles in context of diverse landscapes (coast, mountains, desert, forest)
  • Details that show craftsmanship
  • People enjoying amenities naturally
  • Color grade toward warm sunset tones when appropriate
  • Inclusive representation across age, ethnicity, vehicle type, and travel style

Part 5: Messaging Framework

Core Messaging Hierarchy

Never Lead With Technology

Following Theo Reichelt's proven methodology: benefits first, features second, specs only when asked.

Tier 1: Emotional Benefits (Always Lead)

  • "15-minute scenic breaks worth taking"
  • "Coffee with incredible views while you charge"
  • "The charging stop other drivers recommend"
  • "Where your road trip gets better, not longer"
  • "Charging destinations as memorable as your destination"
  • "Indoor comfort when the weather isn't cooperating"

Tier 2: Functional Benefits (Support the Emotion)

  • "Add 200 miles in the time it takes for a coffee and stretch"
  • "Works every time, guaranteed—or your session is free"
  • "Find us in every app you already use"
  • "Real food, clean facilities, comfortable seating"
  • "Climate-controlled comfort whether it's foggy, windy, or hot"
  • "Regional support teams respond within 2 hours"

Tier 3: Technical Specifications (Only When Asked)

  • "Up to 350kW true power to every port"
  • "Solar + battery backup systems"
  • "NACS and CCS compatible"
  • "99% uptime commitment at Basecamps, 98% at Waystations"
  • "Kempower Satellite charging architecture"
  • "OCPI-integrated for universal app compatibility"

"The only network guaranteeing climate-controlled indoor comfort at EVERY location"

This is our category-defining advantage. While competitors leave drivers exposed to weather in parking lots, Rangeway provides premium indoor experiences whether you're at a staffed Basecamp or an automated Waystation.

Part 6: Customer Journey Marketing

The Five Stages

1. Discovery: "I need charging on scenic routes"

Customer Mindset: Planning a road trip, researching charging options, concerned about infrastructure gaps on scenic highways

Marketing Tactics:

  • SEO content: "Best EV charging for [Route 66/Blue Ridge Parkway/Pacific Coast Highway]"
  • Appear in all navigation apps (Google Maps, Apple Maps, ABRP, PlugShare)
  • Partner content with regional tourism boards
  • Social proof from early users and influencers
  • Geographic-specific landing pages for major scenic corridors
  • YouTube content: "Complete guide to EV charging on [scenic route]"

2. Consideration: "Which charger should I choose?"

Customer Mindset: Comparing options, evaluating reliability and experience, concerned about being stranded

Differentiators to Emphasize:

  • "The only network built FOR scenic routes"
  • "Guaranteed indoor comfort at every location"
  • "Staffed Basecamps and automated Waystations—reliability at both"
  • "Guaranteed to work or your session is free"
  • "99% uptime at Basecamps, 98% at Waystations"
  • User reviews (target 4.8+ stars) and authentic photos
  • Side-by-side comparisons showing our advantage

3. Arrival: "This is actually nice"

Customer Mindset: First impressions matter, comparing reality to expectations, forming initial brand opinion

On-Site Experience Elements:

  • Clear wayfinding signage from highway
  • Welcome messaging on charger screens with personalization
  • Ambient brand touches (curated music, natural materials, regional character)
  • Staff wearing "Trailkeeper" badges at Basecamps (not "technicians")
  • Intuitive wayfinding and signage at automated Waystations
  • Branded photo opportunities with scenic backdrops
  • QR codes for instant app download and account creation

4. Charging: "I could stay longer"

Customer Mindset: Looking for ways to fill dwell time, forming memories and stories, deciding if they'll return

Engagement Tactics:

  • Push notification: "You're at 80%. Your coffee's on us after 10 visits"
  • Instagram-worthy moments: Branded photo spots with scenic views
  • Local discovery: "While you charge" recommendations for nearby attractions within 5-minute walk
  • Real-time charging updates without anxiety-inducing language
  • Tourism partnership QR codes for local attractions
  • Digital screens showing route recommendations and regional highlights
  • At Basecamps: Staff proactively checking in, offering recommendations

5. Advocacy: "You have to stop at Rangeway"

Customer Mindset: Had a surprisingly good experience, wants to share, looking for rewards for referrals

Activation Methods:

  • Share incentives: "Share your #RangewayMoment for 10% off next session"
  • Referral rewards: "Give $10 charging credit, get $10 credit"
  • User-generated content campaigns with monthly prizes
  • Community meetups and events at Basecamps
  • "Rangeway Moments" social series featuring real customers
  • VIP experiences for top advocates (Pioneer tier and above)
  • "Ambassador" program for enthusiastic community members

Part 10: Content Guidelines

Photography Standards

Composition & Style:

  • Golden hour preferred (sunrise/sunset for brand color alignment)
  • Rule of thirds and leading lines emphasizing the journey
  • Authentic moments with real drivers, not models
  • Include vehicle in landscape showing regional character
  • Detail shots: Coffee, amenities, interactions, charger UX
  • Authenticity over perfection: Capture genuine experiences
  • Seasonal variation: Document locations across all four seasons

Subject Diversity:

  • Variety of EV makes and models (not just premium brands)
  • Diverse representation of drivers (age, ethnicity, family composition)
  • Solo travelers, couples, families, friend groups
  • Different travel styles (adventure, luxury, budget, accessibility)
  • Include accessibility features and accessible amenities

Video Guidelines

User Testimonials (30-60 seconds)

  • Focus on emotional experience and surprise/delight
  • Capture spontaneous reactions, not scripted responses
  • Ask: "How was this different from other charging stops?"
  • Include B-roll of them using amenities
  • Natural lighting, minimal production

Site Tours (2-3 minutes)

  • Walkthrough of amenities with narrative
  • Charging process demonstration from arrival to departure
  • Highlight local area and scenic surroundings
  • Interview with Trailkeepers (Basecamps)
  • Show both peak and off-peak experiences

Copy Guidelines

Voice & Tone:

  • Warm and welcoming, not corporate
  • Confident but not arrogant
  • Knowledgeable but not condescending
  • Conversational but professional
  • Optimistic about electric travel

Brand Vocabulary

  • Waystations (automated locations)
  • Trailkeepers (staff at Basecamps)
  • Pathfinders (loyalty members)
  • Scenic corridors/highways

  • Hubs/satellites (outdated terminology)

Part 12: Crisis Communication

Outage Response Protocol

Minute 0-5: Detection & Initial Response

  • Automatic customer notifications via app/SMS
  • Social media status update posted
  • Alternative charger recommendations provided automatically
  • Internal team alert and mobilization
  • Begin diagnostics and response dispatch

Minute 5-30: Ongoing Communication

Standard Message Template:
[Location] Basecamp/Waystation is temporarily offline. 

Estimated restoration: [time]

Nearest alternatives:
• [Alternative 1]: [X] miles → [directions link]
• [Alternative 2]: [Y] miles → [directions link]

Your next charge on us: Affected customers receive 
2 free sessions automatically. No action needed.

We're on it. Updates every 15 minutes.

Post-Resolution: Recovery & Transparency

  • Thank you message to affected customers within 1 hour
  • Root cause analysis shared within 24 hours (when appropriate)
  • Compensation automatically applied (2 free sessions)
  • Process improvement communication
  • Service quality recommitment

Site Delay Protocol

For Construction/Permitting Delays

30+ Days Before Original Opening:
  • Proactive communication to waitlist and local community
  • Explain reason (permitting, weather, supply chain, equipment)
  • Provide revised opening date with confidence level
  • Offer "First to Know" notification signup
  • Share construction progress photos weekly
Community Engagement During Delay:
  • Host preview event when site does open ("worth the wait")
  • Extra celebration at grand opening
  • "Worth the Wait" promotional offer (e.g., 20% off first month)
  • Behind-the-scenes content about challenges overcome

Negative Review Management

Response Protocol:

  1. Acknowledge within 24 hours on all public platforms
  2. Apologize sincerely for the experience
  3. Offer immediate resolution (refund, free sessions, direct contact)
  4. Take conversation offline when appropriate
  5. Follow up publicly once resolved (with permission)
